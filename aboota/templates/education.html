What is Forex Market?

The word Forex consists of two aspects; Foreign currency and Exchange. Foreign Exchange provides the facility to convert one currency into another currency. For Example converting, the US Dollars into the Yen.

Forex Trading

A forex market is a place where buyers and sellers trade in currencies of different countries. It is an OTC market run by global buyers and sellers. You can trade in currency and gain from the fluctuation in the price. Few popular currency pairs are EUR/USD, USD/JYP, GBP/USD, AUD/USD, and USD/CAD. Forex is the world's largest market having a daily turnover of more than 6 trillion dollars. It is considered ideal for beginners willing to get a good exposure with a small investment.

What are Cryptocurrencies?

Cryptocurrency is a digital currency secured by cryptography. Digital or virtual currency refers to a digital existence. It's similar to having money in your bank account, but the only difference is you cannot withdraw it and keep it in your pocket in physical form. But like any other currency, you can trade in cryptocurrency and use it as a mode of exchange. This feature of cryptocurrency makes it a globally accepted currency.

Crypto Trading

You can trade and invest in different cryptocurrencies worldwide. These currencies stay in your digital wallet. You can sell them when you see a suitable price or profits. In the last few years, cryptocurrencies have outperformed all the other global financial instruments. For example, Bitcoin generated a return of 64000 times in just ten years, making the cryptocurrency market most lucrative worldwide. The few most popular currencies are; Bitcoin, Ethereum, Binance, Cardano, Dogecoin, etc.

What are Indices?

Index refers to a group of stocks. Index measures and analyzes the performance of a group of stocks, a sector, or an economy as a whole. A group of stocks put together forms an Index. This group of stocks might be from a specific sector such as Banking, Manufacturing, etc.

Indices Trading

When you trade in any of the indexes, it is called indices trading. Indices trading offers to avoid stock risk by investing in a group of stocks. It also allows you to enjoy the uptrend and downtrend by enabling you to take bi-directional trades. Few popular global indices are FTSE, S&P Global, Dow Jones, NASDAQ, etc. .

What is Commodity?

Commodities are the raw materials used to produce finished goods. Commodities include three broad categories; Metals, Energy, and agriculture. The most popular commodities for trading are Gold, Silver, Platinum, Crude Oil, Copper, etc. .

Commodity Trading

The commodity market is the place to buy and sell commodities like Gold, Silver, Platinum, Energy, Oil, etc. Trading in these commodities is called commodity trading. It offers investors an option to diversify their portfolios. It is a great investment option against an inflationary environment. The commodity market tends to move against the stock market, providing investors to minimize their risk in case of market turmoil.

How To Trade in Global Financial Market

Trading is an art as well as a science. As an art, it requires the skills and understanding to create wealth. And as a science, it implies the analysis of data. For an investor or a trader, it is crucial to know about the availability of trading tools and their uses. Here you will find some of the most popular trading tools and their applications.

Trading Charts

Charts are the graphical presentation of the price and volume of an asset over a period of time. We can predict the future only with the help of the past, and the same applies to the global financial market. Thus, charts are used to understand the past performance of the asset at different points in time. These charts using other indicators, help investors analyze the correct trend and make profits.

Trading Lines

Trend lines are formed by connecting highs or lows of previous swings in the price of an asset. These trend lines, on charts, present a clear picture of the trend and indicate the behavior of the price at some particular levels. Based on these crucial levels, a decision to enter or exit the market becomes more visible. And investors make more or less correct decisions while trading in the global financial market.

Technical indicators

Technical indicators are the signals generated from the price behavior of the assets; based on their demand, supply, and volume analysis. These are mathematical calculations using previous data to predict the trend in the market. Technical indicators, if applied carefully, provide a more or less correct picture of the trends in the global financial market. The few most popular technical indicators are Moving average, Accumulation distribution, MACD, Average Directional Index (ADX), Relative Strength Index (RSI), and Fibonacci. These technical indicators are used in a group to get a correct trend in the market. Investors choose this group as per their trading style and requirements..

Whitepaper

A white paper is an authoritative report or guide that informs readers concisely about a complex issue and presents the issuing body's philosophy on the matter. It is meant to help readers understand an issue, solve a problem, or make a decision.

The initial British term concerning a type of government-issued document has proliferated, taking a somewhat new meaning in business. In business, a white paper is closer to a form of marketing presentation, a tool meant to persuade customers and partners and promote a product or viewpoint, White papers may be considered grey literature.

Since the early 1990s, the term "white paper", or "whitepaper", has been applied to documents used as marketing or sales tools in business.

Fundamental Analysis

Fundamental analysis is a method to find out the intrinsic value of a security by analyzing the economic and financial status of a company or an economy

{% comment %}

Details

{% endcomment %}

The purpose of the fundamental analysis is to calculate the value of a security and compare it with the current price an investor needs to pay. If the intrinsic value of the security is lower than the current market price, investing in the security seems more profitable. The fundamental analysis starts from a macro analysis then moves towards the microanalysis. First, the overall state of the economy is analyzed before moving to a sector or business unit. Fundamental analysis uses open data to evaluate an economy, a sector, or a business. It considers the various factors such as Return on Equity, Revenue, Future growth, Profit margin, Underlying assets, etc.
